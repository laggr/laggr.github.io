HURRICANE KATRINA: TENTH ANNIVERSARY

Problem: Design a logo for a community steeped in the disaster without resorting to cliche.

Process: I transfered the spray-painted X — iconography that was hauntingly familiar to storm survivors in New Orleanians — to typical New Orleans siding; hand drew the date of the storm with the years ’05 and ’15 on either side. The zero, which indicated the number of deaths in a home in the original tagging by military units inspecting homes after the storm, was used to indicate that New Orleans was still standing.

Programs: Photoshop

LOUISIANA PURCHASED

Problem: Create a logo that has impact in print, online and on television.

Process: With the idea that Louisiana was up for purchase to the highest bidder, I designed a logo where the barcode was the shape of the state. I masked an existing barcode with an image of the state. I added the words where the numbers are in barcodes. I created a red to black gradient to show a scanning laser and animated it going down the barcode.

Programs: Illustrator, Photoshop

POPLINGS FOR CASSIDY LABS

Problem: Can packaging and information increase the perceived value of a toy from a parent’s point of view?

Process: Over six weeks in the spring of 2017, I studied Poplings for Cassidy Labs. Observed 18 children (4 via video, 14 in person) and 8 purchasers in natural environment with all four Poplings styles. My findings led to designing a playful logo; making  the local company prominent; giving  Poplings a backstory; creating custom packages including this blister package.
